Glossary

___________

 

Terminology

Batch workload

 

It is constituted by jobs that do not require interaction with the users during their execution. Its intensity is specified by a parameter indicating the average number of executing jobs. A batch workload has a fixed population. Jobs that have completed service can be thought of as leaving and being replaced instantaneously from a backlog of waiting jobs.

 

Bottleneck

 

The bottleneck resource is the first to saturate. In a model with load independent centers, the bottleneck coincides with the resource with the largest service demand (Di).

 

Closed System Models

 

Models in which customers that have completed service leave the system and are instantaneously replaced by a new customer. A closed model has a fixed number of executing customers.

 

Customer

 

Element that flows between resources requiring service. It can be a job, a transaction, an database query, a database update and so on.

 

Delay station

 

Customers at a delay station are each (logically) allocated their own server, so there is no competition for service (no queue). The time spent by a customer at a delay station is exactly that customer’s service demand.

 

Interactive workload (terminal workload)

 

Its intensities specified by two parameters: the number of active terminals (customers), and the average length of time that customers use terminals ("think time") between interactions.

 

Load Dependent Resource (station)

 

A load dependent service station can be thought of as a service station whose service rate (the reciprocal of its service time) is a function of the customer population in its queue.

 

Mean value analysis (MVA)

 

Iterative technique used to evaluate closed queueing networks.

 

Multiple class models

 

Models with several customer classes, each of which has its own workload intensity and its own service demand at each center. Within each class, the customers are indistinguishable.

 

Number of customers at a resource i (Qi)

 

The average queue length at resource i include all customers at that center, whether waiting in queue or receiving service.

 

Number of visits

 

The average number of visits that a customer makes to each resource during a complete execution.

 

Open System Models

 

Models in which there is an infinite stream of arriving customers. The number of customers in the model varies over time and can be infinitely large depending on the congestion level of the resources.

 

Queueing resource

 

Customers at a queueing resource compete for the use of the server. The time spent by a customer at a queueing resource has two components: time spent waiting in queue and time spent receiving service.

 

Queueing network model

 

A network of queues is a collection of service resources, which represent system resources, and customers, which represent users or transactions.

 

Response time (R)

 

Average interval of time between a customer arrival and departure from the system.

 

Saturated resource

 

When the arrival rate at a resource is greater than or equal to the maximum service rate, the resource is said to be saturated (i. e., its utilization is equal to 1).

 

Service Demands (Di)

 

The service demands of a customer at resource i, Di , is the total amount of the time the customer requires in service at that resource.

 

Service time of resource i (Si)

 

Average service requirement per visit at resource i.

 

Single class models

 

Models in which customers are indistinguishable with respect to their service demands. They represent the "average customer" in the system.

 

Throughput (X)

 

Rate at which customers depart from the system (i.e. complete their resource consumption)

 

Transaction workload

 

Its population varies overtime. Customers that have completed service leave the model. Its intensity is specified by a parameter indicating the rate at which requests (customers) arrive at the model.

 

Utilization (U)

 

The utilization of a queueing center is the proportion of time the resource is busy or, equivalently, the average number of customers in service there (definition valid also for delay centers).
