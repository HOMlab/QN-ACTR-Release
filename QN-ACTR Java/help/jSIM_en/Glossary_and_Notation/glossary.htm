Glossary

_________________

 

Terminology

 

 

Batch workload

It is a workload comprising jobs that do not require interaction with the users during their execution. Its intensity is specified by the average number of jobs in execution. A batch workload is composed of a fixed number of elements, i.e., the population is constant. When a job completes its service and leaves the system, it is instantaneously replaced by the next job waiting for service.

 

Bottleneck

The bottleneck resource is the first one to saturate. In a model with load independent service centers, the bottleneck coincides with the resource with the largest service demand (Di).

 

Closed models

Closed models are models in which a customer that has completed service leaves the system and is instantaneously replaced by a new customer. A closed model has a fixed number of executing customers.

 

Customer

Element that flows between resources requiring service. It can represent a job, a transaction, a database query, a database update or any other request of service from a resource.

 

Delay station

A delay station is a special type of server where customers never wait in queue for service. They are immediately served as soon as they arrive at the station, as if the station had an Infinite number of Servers (such stations are often indicated as IS, Infinite Server). The time a customer spends at a delay station is simply the customer's service demand at the station.

 

Interactive workload (terminal workload)

This workload type describes the behavior of a user at a terminal, where some time is spent ("thinking") before submitting the next command to the system for execution, when the results of the previous command are available. Its intensity is specified by two parameters: the number of active terminals (customers) and the average amount of time spent between interactions ("think time").

 

Load dependent resource (station)

A load dependent service station is a service station whose service time is a function of the customer population in station (both in service and waiting in queue).

 

Mean value analysis (MVA)

Iterative technique for the solution of closed queueing network models.

 

Multiclass model

A systemm model where customers can be divided in groups, known as classes, characterized by different parameters such as intensity and service demand at the various stations. Within each class, customers are indistinguishable.

 

Number of customers at a resource i (Qi)

The average number of customers at the resource, including the one(s) in service and those in queue waiting for service.

 

Number of visits at a resource i (Vi)

The average number of times a customer visits a resource in order to complete its execution in the system.

 

Open model

In an open model customers keep arriving as if from an infinite population. The number of customers in the system varies over time and can grow extremely large depending on the congestion level of the resources.

 

Queueing resource (queueing station)

A resource where customers compete for the use of the server. The time a customer spends at a queueing resource comprises the time spent waiting in queue and the time spent receiving service.

 

Queueing network model

A network of queues is a collection of service resources that represent system resources and customers that represent users or transactions.

 

Response time (R)

Average time a customer spends (waiting and being served) for each request at a station.

 

Saturated resource

A resource is saturated, i.e., its utilization is equal to 1, when the arrival rate at a resource is greater than or equal to its maximum service rate.

 

Service demand (Di)

The service demand of a customer at resource i, Di , is the total amount of time a customer spends in service at the resource.

 

Service time of resource i (Si)

Average service requirement per visit at resource i.

 

Single class model

A model where customers are statistically indistinguishable with respect to their service demands. They represent the "average customer" in the system.

 

Throughput (X)

Rate at which customers terminate their execution at the system and leave (i.e., complete their resource consumption).

 

Transaction workload

A transaction workload is comprised of a population that varies over time. Customers leave the system after completing service. Its intensity indicates the rate at which requests (customers) arrive at the system.

 

Utilization (U)

The utilization of a queueing center is the fraction of time the resource is busy or, equivalently, the average number of customers in service at the center (this definition holds for delay centers as well).
